## Results (test set, 689,351 votes on 2,313 unseen future rollcalls)

| model | log loss | acc | contested acc | APRE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| constant_rate | 0.617 | 70.9% | 52.7% | -0.11 |
| meta_tower_8d | 0.511 | 72.3% | 60.7% | -0.05 |
| party_question_rate | 0.466 | 76.6% | 69.8% | 0.11 |
| text_tower_8d | 0.464 | 77.9% | 69.4% | 0.16 |
| member_question_rate | 0.459 | 76.0% | 69.2% | 0.09 |

Findings, reported per SOUL.md (no burying):
- **Bill text works**: identical architecture with vs. without text gains
  0.047 log loss and +5.5pp accuracy. Best accuracy (77.9%) and APRE (0.16)
  of any forecast model.
- **But the fixed primary metric (log loss) is still led by
  member_question_rate** (0.459 vs 0.464) — smoothed empirical rates are
  better calibrated than the tower, and the tower lacks member x question
  interactions. The honest headline: text adds real signal; the text model
  is not yet strictly dominant.
- Forecast regime is far harder than completion: best 0.459/77.9% vs
  Regime A's 0.153/93.8%.
