## Anomalies investigated (the interesting part)

- **Pooled 1D fit placed senators on a flipped axis** (overall corr w/
  NOMINATE 0.40). Diagnosis: House and Senate share no rollcalls; the two
  blocks' relative orientation is identified only through ~95
  chamber-switchers. House-only corr was +0.97, Senate-only −0.86,
  switchers crushed toward 0. Fix: fit per chamber for interpretation.
- **Senate-only fit then showed corr ≈ 0.01** — diagnosed as a *twisted
  axis*: within-congress correlation with NOMINATE moved smoothly from
  −0.95 (101st) to +0.95 (119th). A local optimum with equal predictive fit:
  orientation is unidentified, and SGD let it rotate across eras. Fix:
  party-sign initialization (+0.5 R / −0.5 D), which pins orientation but
  not within-party ordering (so validation against NOMINATE is not circular).
- After fixes: **House corr 0.979, Senate corr 0.978; within-party
  0.84–0.91.** Largest remaining disagreements are substantively sensible:
  Flake, Ron Paul, Rand Paul, Stockman — libertarians/mavericks that 1D
  NOMINATE also fits poorly.
- Methodological lesson logged: the benchmark rewards prediction, but
  interpretation requires explicit identification constraints. This goes
  into the eventual paper's methods section.
