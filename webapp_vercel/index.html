Bill Vote Forecaster

Paste a bill's summary — a paragraph or two describing what it does. A frozen language-model-based model from Reading the Bills forecasts how every member of the current (119th) Congress would vote on final passage, and where the bill would divide the chamber. Every number is a model prediction, not an observed outcome.

Try: a bipartisan tax bill a gun-safety bill a border-security bill a climate bill
Or paste a real one: browse recently introduced bills on GovTrack (or search Congress.gov), open one, and copy its summary — the plain-language description, not the full legislative text.

Forecasts come from the hash-pinned frozen artifact described in the paper (MiniLM-tower component), scored for a final-passage rollcall in the 119th Congress. The cutpoint is on the 119th-Congress ideal-point scale (negative = liberal). The model reads only the pasted text plus the sponsoring party; it has never seen the bill. It was trained on plain-language bill summaries and reads a short (~250-word) window, so a summary works better than raw legislative text. Off-agenda inputs are flagged.
